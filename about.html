About

The Hawai‘i Names Project documents and organizes the origins, meanings, and histories behind the names of places that shape our islands.

From monarchy and missionaries to plantation towns, military sites, surf breaks, and places we've lost, we explore how names reflect power, memory, identity, and change.

The goal is to create a living, evolving map of Hawaiʻi’s memory grounded in research, accessible to the public, and built to last.

New pins, Places We Lost, Place Name Profiles, and Stories are added regularly.
